## Required Rechecks

1. **Sixth cervical vertebra articulation claim:** The statement that the sixth cervical vertebra has a specialized articulation enabling the S-curve strike mechanism is supported by Sibley and the creation.com article, but a peer-reviewed anatomical study should be located for independent verification. The recent PMC article on heron cervical vertebrae (PMC13014071) may provide this.

2. **Pleistocene *Ardea herodias* fossil specimens:** The Smithsonian specimens (USNM PAL210282, PAL210285) from Wakulla County are listed in online catalogs, but their precise stratigraphic context and dating should be verified through the primary paleontological literature.

3. **Grande Coupure and heron dispersal:** The suggestion that herons arrived in Europe as part of the Grande Coupure faunal turnover is an inference from the timing of the earliest fossils. Mayr et al. (2019) note this correlation but do not claim causation. The chapter text should be reviewed to ensure this distinction is clear.
